The Whitney effect

People name their kids after popstars

By Lisa Lind

It's not an easy decision - to find the perfect name for your baby. Often names are passed down in the family from generation to generation - there are also names that remain popular for decades and then there are trends.

For this homework exercise, I took a closer look at baby names in the USA since 1880 and tried to find out if there is a trend towards naming your own children after pop stars. The result is clear: yes, there is.

Looking at the most successful popstars since the early 1980s according to the Billboard charts, it is clear that there is a Whitney Houston effect.

In fact, the name Whitney barely appears until the early 80s. However, this changed with Whitney Houston's rise to superstar. The situation is similar with names like Madonna, Mariah (Carey) or Britney (Spears).

There are even names that pop up all at once - such as Miley.

The same can also be seen with the names Rihanna, Beyonc√© or Ariane (Grande).

Names that were once popular decades ago can also regain popularity through popstars - such as the name Adele.

To be able to recognize an effect, the name should not be too common, which is the case with many male popstars. Only with Kanye a trend can be recognized.

This effect also exists in Austria. Here it is often referred to as the "Kevin-effect". One reason why the name suddenly became so popular in the 1990s is probably the movie "Home Alone" with Macaulay Culkin.

In comparison, you don't see this effect in the USA because Kevin has been popular here for much longer.

Whether it makes sense to name children after popstars or movies is a decision parents have to make. However, there are now studies in Austria and Germany, for example, which show that children named Kevin are given lower marks at school by their teachers than others because the name has probably fallen into disrepute.
